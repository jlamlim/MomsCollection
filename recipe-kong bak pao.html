Kong Bak Pao

Stuff You Need
Spices Comments
3 Star Anise Read that we should avoid the Japanese Star Anise. Get the Chinese Star Anise.
2 Cinnamom Sticks About 8 cm per in length.
White Peppercorns 2 tablespoons, lightly crushed.
Galangal (Blue Ginger) Around 8 cm in length, skin and slice it into 0.25 cm thick.
6-8 pieces Cloves
6 pieces of garlic Crush them lightly.
Other Ingredients Comments
1/2 kg of Pork Belly I usually ask my butcher to give me the leaner cut.
Thick Dark Soya Sauce Like the ones served with Chicken Rice and Bak Kut Teh. These are not that salty and adds a strong color to the dish.
Light Soya Sauce To be added towards the end of cooking according to preference. It all depends on the saltiness of the Dark Soya Sauce.
Brown Rock Sugar Add to taste. You can replace with white rock sugar but I prefer brown sugar as I think it gives the dish a richer flavor.
Chinese Rice Wine This further aromatize the dish. Add according to preference. I usually add about 2-3 tablespoons.
Corn Starch To thicken the gravy at the end of cooking.
1 tablespoon Cooking Oil We just need enough to fry the spices.
Water Sufficient to cover the Pork Belly as it stews.
Cooking Time
  1. Blanch the Pork Belly, then rinse it in cold water to remove residual grime. Cut the Pork Belly into pieces of 8 cm wide and 1.5 cm thick.

  2. Generously marinade the sliced Pork Belly in the Thick Dark Soya Sauce and leave it standing at room temperature for 1 hour.

  3. Fry the spices till fragrant.

  4. Add the marinated Pork Belly and give the Pork Belly some browning.

  5. Add Brown Sugar and simmer the Pork Belly enough water to over them. You would want to make sure that you have sufficent gravy to serve with the meat after cooking. You can add in more Thick Dark Soya Sauce if you think the dish looks too pale..

  6. When the meat is almost tender to your liking, add Light Soya Sauce and more Brown Sugar to taste. Continue cooking to the doneness you prefer.

  7. When the meat is cooked, put aside the meat from the sauce. Strain the sauce to remove the spices. This will make it easier to enjoy the dish without having to bite into something awlful.

  8. Put back the meat into the sauce and heat up the dish again. You can now add in the Chinese Rice Wine and thicken the sauce with Corn Starch.

  9. You can then serve the dish with the pao for Kong Bak Pao or with rice or with lettuce - the way the Koreans enjoy their meat. I like to garnish them with coriander and with loads of sliced chilli padi.
